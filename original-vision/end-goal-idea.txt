End Goal: Function-Based CAD Prompting
======================================

The ideal AI-for-CAD interaction is function/requirement-driven, not geometry-driven.

Example prompt:
  "Design a rotational-to-planar motion mechanism that fits in a 5x5x5cm box,
   is reliable for 100,000 cycles, and can sustain 50N lateral force."

What this requires the AI to do:
- Understand the functional requirement (motion type, constraints)
- Select an appropriate mechanism (cam, rack-and-pinion, scotch yoke, etc.)
- Break it into components with correct geometry + tolerances
- Reason about material properties, manufacturing constraints
- Output actual CAD geometry or assembly

Why this is hard:
- Mechanism selection is a design space search problem
- Physics constraints need to propagate through geometry
- Tolerancing, assembly fits, material choice all interact
- Current AI-for-CAD tools skip all of this and go straight to geometry

This is the real "AI Mechanical Engineer" problem. CAD generation is a small
downstream step — the hard part is mechanism design and constraint reasoning.
