|---|---|---|

4. If good reception, expand for NeurIPS D&B track or ICLR 2027

## General Notes

- **arXiv always first** — establishes priority, can be cited before formal acceptance
- **Workshop papers are underrated** — they get you in front of the exact people you want to meet, often lead to collaborations, and are much faster to publish
- **CoRL is underrated for this work** — robotics community thinks about assembly, physical feedback, and spatial reasoning exactly in the way this thesis requires. Less competitive than NeurIPS/ICLR, more targeted audience.
- **Faculty co-author helps a lot** — especially for first submission. Adds credibility, they know the reviewers, and they can flag framing issues before submission.
